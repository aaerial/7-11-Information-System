About us

7-Eleven Philippines is operated by Philippine Seven Corporation (PSC), which follows a franchise model emphasizing shared success between the franchisor and franchisees. This model provides comprehensive support in areas such as product development, store maintenance, and bookkeeping services, allowing franchisees to focus primarily on daily operations and customer engagement.

The 7-Eleven store located at Ayala Cebu, Samar Loop, is one of the franchised branches of this globally recognized convenience store chain. Uniquely, this branch is owned and operated by three entrepreneurial sisters who ventured into the retail industry together. Their partnership reflects strong family values and a collaborative approach to business, dedicated to delivering quality service and convenience to their community.

This branch aligns closely with 7-Eleven Philippines’ mission: “To make daily life easier by providing modern convenience,” and vision: “To be the best retailer of convenience for emerging markets.” These guiding principles ensure consistent service quality and a reliable customer experience across all 7-Eleven branches.

The Hop-Inn branch at Ayala Cebu is managed by one store manager and supported by a team of 12 workers who rotate in shifts to ensure smooth and continuous store operations.

Statement of the Problem

The inventory management system at the selected 7-Eleven branch has faced challenges in maintaining optimal stock levels, resulting in frequent product shortages over the past three months. These shortages have led to missed sales opportunities, negatively affecting both customer satisfaction and overall store performance.

Although the branch uses an automated replenishment system, it does not consistently reflect real-time demand or quickly adapt to changes in customer purchasing behavior. As a result, the store often runs out of fast-moving items.

This study aims to investigate the root causes of these inventory shortages and propose improvements to the replenishment and stock monitoring processes to minimize stockouts and enhance product availability in the store.

Ishikawa Diagram

To determine the underlying causes of inventory imbalances at the 7-Eleven Hop Inn Cebu branch, a Fishbone Diagram (Ishikawa Diagram) was used to categorize potential factors under six major areas: Man, Machine, Materials, Method, Measurement, and Environment. This analysis highlights the need for integrated system improvements, better training, and a more adaptive inventory approach to ensure product availability and minimize waste.
